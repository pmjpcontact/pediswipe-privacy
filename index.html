Privacy Policy

PediSwipe — Pediatric Emergency Dosing for Apple Watch

Last updated: March 12, 2026

Summary

PediSwipe does not collect or store any personal data. The app runs entirely on your device. An optional feedback feature allows you to voluntarily send a message to the developer (see below).

Data Collection

PediSwipe does not collect personal data. There are no user accounts, no analytics, no tracking, no cookies, and no third-party SDKs.

Data Storage

All app data (such as settings and preferences) is stored locally on your device and is never transmitted to any server.

Optional Feedback

PediSwipe includes an optional feedback feature. If you choose to send feedback, only the message text you write, the source (Watch or iPhone), and a timestamp are transmitted to our server for support purposes. No device identifiers, personal information, or clinical data are included. Feedback is voluntary and the app functions fully without it.

Third-Party Services

PediSwipe does not integrate with any third-party advertising networks, analytics platforms, or data brokers. The optional feedback feature uses a private webhook endpoint operated by the developer.

Children's Privacy

PediSwipe is a clinical reference tool intended for healthcare professionals. It does not collect data from any users, including children.

Medical Disclaimer

PediSwipe is a clinical reference tool and does not replace professional medical judgment. Always consult a physician and verify all dosing information against current guidelines before making medical decisions. The developer assumes no liability for clinical decisions made using this app.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy is updated, the revised version will be posted at this URL with an updated date.
